Description
Inland Taipan
  It's paralyzing venom consists of taipoxin, a mix of neurotoxins, procoagulants, and Myotoxins.
Eastern Brown Snake
  It's considered the world's second-most venomous land snake and is responsible for about 60% of snakebite deaths in Australia.
Coastal Taipan
  It's produces venom that is almost identical to that of the inlan taipan - considered to be the most venomous snake in the world.
King Cobra
  The King Cobra is the world's longest venomous snake, growing growing up to 5.5 meters in length.
Black Mamba
  Often regarded as the fastest snake in the world, it's actually beaten into second position by the sidewinder.
